SER120 Projects

Project 3: Swing App

Create a program using layout managers, UI elements such as JButton and JLabel, and a custom drawing panel class.

In-Class Project

This is intended as an in-class project. We will have time to work on it during most classes up until the deadline, and you should review your work with me during class before submitting it on Blackboard.

Group Work

You may work on this project with a partner under the following conditions:

  1. You inform me that you are working together by Thursday, 4/10.
  2. Both you and your partner complete the majority of your work on the project together during class.
  3. You show me your completed work in class or office hours before submitting.

Primary Topics

  • Interfaces
  • Swing and AWT
    • Layouts
    • Event listeners
    • Drawing with java.awt.Graphics

Tasks

Task 1: Design

Design a layout for your program. The layout must include at least two buttons or other interactive UI elements from the swing library, it must dedicate a large area to the drawing panel, and it must use at least two layout managers other than FlowLayout.

You must show me the material listed below during class and submit it on Blackboard by 11:59 PM on Monday, April 14th.

  • A sketch or mockup of your program
  • An illustration of how layout managers will be used to create the program
  • A sketch of mockup of what the drawing panel will display
  • A description of how at least one swing UI element (JButton, JSlider, etc.) will affect the drawing panel
  • A description of at least one mouse interaction that will affect the drawing panel
  • The descriptions above must include a total of four interactions, divided as you wish between UI elements and mouse
  • You may list additional mouse and UI element interactions separately, but you will not be required to implement them in Part 2

I may allow compromises when reviewing designs in class as long as they're not meant to trivialize the project (for example, using several regular jpanels instead of a single drawing panel). If you do not show me your design during class, then you will not receive feedback until after the deadline or be allowed to resubmit if the design is incomplete.

Finally, note that I expect you to design your own program and draw/write the design yourself. Your design must not be based on a classmate's design, and you are not allowed to use ChatGPT or another AI/LLM to assist with the design.

Task 2: Program

Implement the program in your design. Your program must appear as shown in your design and include all four of the interactions described in the design.

You must show me your working program in class and submit it on Blackboard by 11:59 PM ET on Wednesday, April 23rd.

Deliverables

  • Part 1: all design files, preferably in PDF and/or PNG format
  • Part 2: all java files required for the program to run

Submission

Before submitting this assignment, make sure you understand and abide by the Academic Integrity policy in our syllabus. This is particularly important if you received any assistance, made use of an LLM, or used resources that were not provided in class.

In order for the assignment to count as complete:

  • Your design must
    • include all visuals and descriptions listed in the design section
    • meet all requirements (number of interactions, layouts, etc.) in the design section
    • include mockups that are consistent and achievable with the proposed layout managers
  • Your program must
    • be consistent with the visual mockups in the design
    • use the layouts as proposed in the design
    • implement the four required interactions described in the design
