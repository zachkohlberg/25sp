Academic Integrity Violations

This section covers how I typically handle AI violations. The full procedure is covered in the university's AI policy, so this will just cover details relevant to our class and my own habits.

Investigation

If I believe that you have committed an AI violation or that your work was used in an AI violation, then I will contact you to ask for more information after I've gathered the necessary evidence. I don't like to pursue suspected violations unless I see conclusive evidence that a violation took place, so you are not likely to convince me of anything by lying at this point.

If you did not violate the AI policy, then this is your opportunity to clear any suspicion by providing additional evidence and letting me know about any work you have shared with other students. Knowing the original author of plagiarized work allows me to argue that the original author does not bear responsibility for the violation due to the Sharing Work section of this syllabus. If you decide to lie about sharing work and later claim to have shared that work in good faith, then I will not believe you.

If you have violated the AI policy, then it's probably too late to avoid getting in trouble once I contact you about the violation. You can cooperate, in which case we can discuss the AI process and attempt a joint resolution (if allowed) after I file the report. If you appear to be lying or decide not to cooperate, then I'm not going to continue talking to you about the violation and will not likely consider a joint resolution, even if you later decide to accept responsibility for the violation.

Sanctions

If you are found responsible for violating the AI policy, then one or more sanctions will be imposed based on the violation(s) for which you are responsible. Below are some guidelines for sanctions that take into account the grading system used in this class.

  • If some or all of an assignment a student submitted submitted wasn't their own work, then they should not receive any credit for the assignment. Individual assignments are complete or incomplete, and there are no inbetween grades. If treating the assignment as incomplete is the only sanction, then we are handling the violation the same way I would handle a student submitting unfinished work and failing to correct the submission before the deadline. Unless the violation is deemed unintentional, this is probably not a sufficient sanction on its own.
  • A student's final grade may be reduced or replaced with an F/FAI depending on the severity of the violation. I would normally lower a final grade by one letter per assignment on which a student cheated after accounting for these assignments being incomplete.
